Privacy Score Methodology

How we calculate privacy safety scores for apps and services

Score Breakdown

Data Collection 25%

What types and volume of personal data does the service collect? Includes device data, behavioral tracking, biometric data, and contact information harvesting.

Third-Party Sharing 25%

Does the service share or sell user data to third parties? Evaluates advertising partnerships, data broker relationships, and cross-platform data sharing.

Security Measures 20%

What security infrastructure protects user data? Covers encryption standards, two-factor authentication, vulnerability response times, and independent security audits.

Transparency 15%

How clearly does the service communicate its data practices? Evaluates privacy policy readability, transparency reports, open-source code, and data request disclosures.

User Control 15%

How much control do users have over their own data? Covers data export, deletion options, granular permission controls, opt-out mechanisms, and consent management.

How Scores Are Calculated

Each app and service is evaluated across our five core criteria. Trained privacy analysts review publicly available privacy policies, terms of service, independent audit reports, and real-world data practices. Scores are updated quarterly or whenever significant policy changes are detected.

Our methodology is informed by privacy scoring and benchmarking research, especially Liu and Terzi's privacy score framework for online social networks, Maass et al.'s PrivacyScore system for public benchmarking, Niu et al.'s framework for estimating mobile app privacy risk scores, and Marono et al.'s quantitative framework for mobile application privacy measurement. Each criterion is scored from 0 to 100 independently and then weighted to produce the final Privacy Safety Score.

Score Ranges

71 — 100
Good — Strong privacy protections. The service demonstrates a genuine commitment to user privacy with minimal data collection and robust security.
40 — 70
Fair — Moderate privacy concerns. The service collects significant data but offers some user controls and security measures.
0 — 39
Poor — Significant privacy risks. The service engages in extensive data collection, aggressive sharing, and provides limited user control.

Continuous Monitoring

Privacy scores are living values — they change as companies update their policies, experience data breaches, or improve their practices. PrivacyPal continuously monitors news, regulatory actions, and policy updates to ensure our scores reflect the current state of each service's privacy posture.
